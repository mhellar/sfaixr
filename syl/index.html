Extended Reality and Immersive Worlds

XR or Extended Reality is a blanket term for a spectrum of immersive technologies that synthesize virtual worlds and merge them with real ones, including virtual reality, augmented reality, and mixed reality. In this class, we will explore this spectrum and develop techniques to craft immersive realities from scratch. This is a technical class, and students will develop a substantial understanding of programming techniques for the WebXR platform from the ground up. We will combine 2D/3D graphics, 360 video, photogrammetry, spatial audio and interactivity, to create immersive, interactive virtual and augmented reality experiences.

VR Flight Hours

1. Throughout the semester, try as many VR experiences as possible. Use the classes “VR Pieces List” or find works on your own (eg sources: VR festival sites, Steam, itch.io).

2. Take notes on each experience you tried. What makes it a (un)successful VR piece? What was your bodily experience of it?

3. Count the cumulative time you’ve spent in the headset. These are your VR Flight Hours.

4. Keep a journal of experiences, observations and total VR flight hours at the end of the quarter for a 10% grade bonus.
